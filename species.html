Bear Species

Explore the Diverse World of Bears

Brown Bear

Ursus arctos

The most widely distributed bear, found across North America, Europe, and Asia. Includes grizzly bears and Kodiak bears.

Least Concern 150-700 kg

Polar Bear

Ursus maritimus

The largest land carnivore, perfectly adapted to Arctic life. Their white fur provides camouflage on ice and snow.

Vulnerable 350-700 kg

American Black Bear

Ursus americanus

North America's most common bear. Highly adaptable, found in forests from Canada to Mexico.

Least Concern 40-250 kg

Asian Black Bear

Ursus thibetanus

Known for the distinctive white V-shaped marking on their chest. Excellent climbers found in Asian forests.

Vulnerable 40-200 kg

Giant Panda

Ailuropoda melanoleuca

China's beloved icon. Despite being a bear, they have a diet of 99% bamboo. Conservation success story.

Vulnerable 70-125 kg

Sun Bear

Helarctos malayanus

The smallest bear species with a distinctive golden crescent on their chest. Native to Southeast Asian rainforests.

Vulnerable 25-65 kg

Sloth Bear

Melursus ursinus

Named for their slow movements. Specialized for eating insects with long, curved claws and a flexible snout.

Vulnerable 55-140 kg

Spectacled Bear

Tremarctos ornatus

South America's only bear, named for distinctive facial markings. Live in Andean cloud forests.

Vulnerable 35-200 kg

Species Quick Facts

Largest

Polar bears are the largest, with males weighing up to 700 kg (1,500 lbs).

Smallest

Sun bears are the smallest at just 25-65 kg, about the size of a large dog.

Fastest

Despite their size, bears can run up to 35 mph (56 km/h).

Strongest

A brown bear's bite is 2,500 psi, stronger than a tiger or lion.
